What are Dental Implants?

Dental Implant is a surgery-based treatment that helps to replace your missing teeth with artificial teeth that appear to be natural teeth. In a Dental Implant surgery, the surgeon infuses stain-resistant materials and is paired with natural-looking crowns for a more youthful look. They improve your appearance and self-esteem. Dental implants can restore the structural integrity of your face. Dental Implants will enhance your eating and speech abilities.
These artificial implants infused by the surgeon hold a replacement tooth or bridge. The implant looks natural in its own way and also doesn’t affect the nearby teeth and has great stability. Osseointegration is the process of fusion between dental implant and Jawbone. In this process the dental implant tends to stimulate the bone to form, this helps it to last much longer. Our dental surgeons mainly opt for titanium, as it allows the implant to integrate with the bone without being recognized as a foreign object in our body. Over time with medical and dental advancements the outcomes of dental implants have drastically improved with a success rate close to 98% in the surgery and treatment.



What should you choose - Dentures or Dental Implants?

Dentures are the most affordable treatment option for the restoration of teeth. The dentist places a removable appliance in the mouth that can be taken out to brush and clean. But there is a big list of disadvantages with these Dentures and that is the reason why expert Dentists do not recommend them.
Dentures tend to sunk the cheek and affect bone loss. Due to space between the denture and oral tissue, it tends to increase bacteria. Dentures also affect the taste buds and speech of a person which indeed is an embarrassment in social gatherings. As patients cannot eat certain kinds of foods they develop malnutrition issues as well. Also, these teeth have to be realigned or restructured after a few years.



Dental Implants benefits

Compared to dentures dental implants are the best option as the prosthetic looks natural and stays for life with very minor dental checkups.
Implants neither affect your teeth nor your taste buds which indeed helps you to lead a healthy routine. As these implants blend with the bone over a period there is no issue with any bacteria formation. Once the treatment is completed dentists claim that patients can get back to their routine within a span of 3-6 months which is a great opportunity for a healthy lifestyle.

Types of Dental Implants

3D Guided Implant

Our 3D Guided Implant surgery is a unique treatment technique that takes Digital planning a step forward. It is an additional procedure that helps to visualize the steps of your implant procedure as well as creates custom drilling templates. 3D guided implant surgery is a treatment combined with CT Scanning technology. CBCT scan is not only recommended by the dentist but the best course of action when planning dental implants for a patient. The scan produces 3D cross-sectional images of the jaws and teeth which provide detailed information about the area that cannot be obtained just with a normal x-ray. With the help of these scans, a dentist can place the dental implants accurately.

All-on-4 Implants

Teeth on 4 implants commonly are called all-on- 4 dental implants. These Implants provide patients with a full arch prosthesis that is installed permanently. In dental treatment, it is used for patients with full dentures who have all their teeth extracted and are not happy with them, particularly lower dentures. Many patients are extremely uncomfortable and lose a lot of confidence because of missing teeth. All on 4 Dental Implants are considered to be the best alternative to dentures or deteriorated teeth.

All-on-6 Implants

The All-On-6 Dental Implant procedure creates a permanent prosthesis by using six dental implants. It acts as anchors for a bridge or over-denture. In this dental procedure, 6 implants are positioned in the lower or upper jawbone to anchor prosthetic teeth in place permanently. The dentist will then evaluate anatomical structures in the mouth with the help of digital x-rays and a 3D Cone Beam CT-Scan. Our dentist will then assess bone loss and create impressions to generate a new smile for you! Over time, the implants positioned in the jaw will fuse with the jawbone, representing the natural roots and giving back a natural smile.
