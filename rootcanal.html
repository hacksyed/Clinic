What is the Root Canal?

Root canal therapy is intended to remove bacteria from infected root canals, prevent contamination of the tooth, and preserve the original tooth. When a root canal is performed, the swollen or diseased pulp is removed, and the inside of the tooth is carefully cleaned and disinfected before being filled and sealed.



Does a root canal hurt?

Getting a root canal isn't much more painful than getting a filling or having a wisdom tooth extracted, since anesthesia is used. After a root canal, the tooth can be sore or numb for a day or two, and this can even cause mild discomfort. Fortunately, modern root canal treatment is nothing like that old saying! There is very little difference between this procedure and a routine filling, and it can be completed in one or two appointments, depending on the condition of the tooth. A root canal is a relatively painless and extremely effective surgery. You'll be smiling, biting, and eating again in no time.

You must keep your new teeth clean and clear from bacteria and plaque for your implants to last long. They can induce infection in the tissues and are the most common causes of implant failure. The maintenance of proper oral hygiene cannot be overstated.



Effective hygienic tips include:

  • Pain when chewing or biting.
  • Hot or cold sensitivity that persists after the sensation has been removed.
  • You may have swollen or tender gums.
  • Deep decay or darkening of the gums.
  • The gums have pus-filled bumps.
  • Cracked or chipped teeth.

Microscopic Root Canal:

Microscopically assisted root canals allow for the observation and treatment of dental disorders that are not visible to the naked eye. They allow the visualization and magnification of structures 25 times the size of their original size.The fundamental benefit of employing the Microscopic Root Canal is its unrivalled accuracy. It is less intrusive, destroys less tooth structure, and allows clinicians to detect and treat root canal anatomy that would otherwise be invisible to the naked eye.The procedure's accuracy and predictability have improved. The documentation is the most crucial element; images of each phase of the root canal treatment can be taken and shared with the patient.

How much does a root canal cost?

Depending on how complex the problem is and which tooth is affected, the cost varies. Molars are more difficult to treat; the fee is generally higher. Many dental insurance policies cover some endodontic treatments.



Re-Root Canal:

With a success rate of more than 95%, root canal treatment is typically safe and effective. A root canal, like any other medical or dental operation, can occasionally fail. This is usually caused by a loose crown, a fractured tooth, or new decay. Root canals can fail weeks or even years after the treatment.


Symptoms to look out for:



  • When biting down, there's a lot of sensitivity.
  • A pimple or boil appears on the jaw.
  • Tooth discoloration.
  • Tenderness in the gum tissue around the root canal.
  • Pain is occurring in the tooth that was fixed.
  • A pus-filled abscess near a treated tooth.

During re-treatment, your dentist will:



  • Remove the crown or filling to gain access to the tooth.
  • RCT should be free of any existing content.
  • Clean the wound and reapply the antimicrobial dressing.
  • Fill the Root Canal with water.
  • Finish the procedure by resealing it.
