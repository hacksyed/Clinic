What Are Braces?

Braces are dental appliances that treat issues with your teeth such as crowding, crooked teeth, and out-of-alignment teeth. Braces are commonly worn by teens. However, they are also used by adults. Braces gradually straighten and align your teeth so you have a normal bite when you wear them. Some people use braces to straighten their teeth.
If you have crooked teeth and/or a misaligned bite (an underbite or overbite), braces and retainers, custom-made, removable or fixed aids that cover the exterior of your teeth and assist keep them in place, are two options. Find out how to determine whether you have an overbite.
Many general dentists may do basic tooth alignment and handle other dental abnormalities, but orthodontists specialize in teeth correction. A dentist goes on to study and train as an orthodontist for two to three years beyond dental school. Dr Jaydev specializes in tooth straightening, bite correction, and jaw disorders. Our orthodontist will ask you questions about your health, do a clinical exam, digitally scan your teeth, take photographs of your face and teeth, and order mouth and head X-rays. Based on this information, we will prepare a therapy strategy.



What Foods Should You Avoid During Braces Treatment?

Braces are a delicate accessory. If a piece of the appliance breaks, the teeth may move in the wrong direction, requiring more treatment time. Anything firm, sticky, or chewy should be avoided, such as:


  • Ice-cream
  • Nuts
  • Popcorn
  • Candy that is hard to break
  • Gum chewing
  • Caramel-like chewy candy
  • Gummies
  • Foods that are hard or difficult to bite, such as apples or bagels.
  • Pretzels made of hard dough
  • Carrots


How Long Will I Have to Wear Braces?

The amount of time it takes to get braces differs from person to person. It is dependent on:

  • What is the severity of your problem?
  • The quantity of space you have in your mouth
  • How well do you follow directions?
  • The length of time it takes for your teeth to travel
  • The teeth, gums, and supporting bone health

Braces are typically worn for one to three years after they are placed. For the first six months after braces are removed, most people must wear a retainer at all times. After that, you must only wear it when sleeping, but you can do so for many years.



Types of Braces:

Children's braces are the most frequent way to straighten their teeth and correct misaligned bites. They aren't like the shining metal mouthfuls of old. Today, there are a lot more options. Your dentist or orthodontist will construct a unique appliance for you if braces are the best solution for you. The braces may include bands, wires, and other fixed or detachable corrective devices. There is no one-size-fits-all solution that will work for everyone.

Metal braces versus conventional braces:

Metal braces are used in traditional braces. They consist of brackets affixed to the front of your teeth or bands that fit around each tooth, as well as flexible wires called archwires that connect the brackets or bands. Rubber bands or metal ties that connect the brackets to the wire are sometimes included with braces. These bands apply additional pressure to your teeth to help them straighten and align. Your orthodontist may need you to wear a nighttime appliance known as headgear. It helps to straighten your teeth by applying additional pressure. It is simple to put on and remove.

Ceramic braces:

Traditional braces' brackets are now fashioned of tooth-colored ceramic, making them less noticeable. Stainless steel, transparent materials, and gold can also be used.

Lingual braces:

These braces have brackets on the backs of your teeth that face your tongue. Lingual braces are more difficult to notice.

Clear aligners:

Invisible braces is another term for them. These are translucent plastic trays that fit over your teeth and are comfortable to wear. They utilize gentle pressure to straighten your smile and shift your teeth into the proper locations. You can take the aligners out to eat, brush, or floss, but they must be worn for at least 22 hours each day to be effective. To keep your aligners in place, your orthodontist may use tooth-colored attachments. Learn more about dental aligners and how to use them at home to straighten your teeth

After Braces

The orthodontist will carefully clean your teeth when your braces are removed. They may want to take further X-rays and bite imprints to see how well the braces worked and if you've developed any wisdom teeth. If your wisdom teeth start to erupt after your braces are gone, your dentist or orthodontist may recommend that you have them extracted to keep your newly aligned teeth from slipping.

A retainer, which is a vital aspect of post-braces maintenance, will be fitted by your dentist or orthodontist. Even if braces have effectively straightened your teeth, the bones, gums, and muscles must adjust to the alteration before they are entirely established in their new position. Teeth also have a tendency to move over time. As a result, the length of time a retainer should be worn varies from person to person.

How Much Do Braces Cost?

The cost varies based on the amount of treatment done, the type of braces used, and where you reside, but standard metal braces should cost between INR 35000 and 55000.
